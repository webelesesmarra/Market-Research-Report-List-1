<p><p>Health and critical illness insurance can be categorized into fixed-term and whole-life insurance markets. Fixed-term insurance provides coverage for a specified duration, typically offering lower premiums and direct payouts for critical illnesses diagnosed during the term. In contrast, whole-life insurance remains active for the policyholder's lifetime, accumulating cash value and ensuring lifelong protection. This type often has higher premiums but also includes a savings component. Both markets aim to provide financial security during health crises, adjusting to the policyholder's needs.</p></p>
