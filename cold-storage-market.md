<p><p>Cold storage markets can be classified into three types: private, semi-private, and public markets. Private cold storage operates independently, catering to specific businesses that require dedicated space for perishables, ensuring tailored services and heightened security. Semi-private facilities serve multiple clients while offering shared resources, balancing cost-effectiveness with some level of exclusivity. In contrast, public cold storage markets operate on an open-access model, allowing any business to store goods, promoting competition and flexibility but with less personalized service.</p></p>
